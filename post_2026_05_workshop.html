BioMonI Activities: Canary Island Biodiversity Workshops

As part of our ongoing commitment to building a global long-term monitoring network (BioMonI-PLOT), we recently convened two coordinated workshops at the University of La Laguna in Tenerife on May 20 and 21 to deeply explore the present status and future trajectory of biodiversity within the Canary Islands. These sessions were structured around two complementary temporal scales:

Workshop 1: Canary Island Biodiversity Today
This session focused on the archipelago’s present status. We evaluated how biodiversity is currently monitored on the Canary Islands and identified the specific challenges and needs for ongoing initiatives. Furthermore, we discussed how experience from the Canary Islands can actively contribute to global island biodiversity research.

Workshop 2: The Future of Canary Island Biodiversity
This session looked ahead to emerging environmental pressures and future trajectories. Key discussions centered on why biodiversity is fundamentally important to the people of the Canary Islands, how it can be safe-guarded for future generations, and identifying where and how Canary Island nature should be protected and utilized in a sustainable way.

The workshops successfully brought together experts across the archipelago to network, exchange knowledge, and develop an in-depth understanding of critical local challenges. Collectively, these discussions helped shape strategies for a future where both biodiversity and society can thrive, while firmly aligning conservation in the Canaries with global initiatives.
